About FLAG

Faruqi Legal Action Group (FLAG) is built on high-quality work product, careful issue-spotting, and practical advocacy. We approach matters the way serious firms do—organize the record, identify leverage, clarify risks, and execute a plan.

Clients come to FLAG for professional communication, disciplined drafting, and negotiation strategy that is calm, controlled, and effective.

Services

FLAG offers practical legal services designed for real-world needs—clear documents, disciplined negotiation, and well-organized strategy. Click a service to view details.
Agreements designed to perform under pressure: scope, payment protections, deliverables, change orders, termination, remedies, indemnity, and dispute provisions—written clearly and built to reduce friction and prevent costly surprises.
Formation and operating documents structured to protect relationships and preserve value: roles, ownership, decision rights, deadlock planning, buy-sell triggers, confidentiality, and exit strategy.
Strong written advocacy and escalation strategy—demand letters, responses, settlement proposals, and record-building communications designed to preserve leverage and move matters toward resolution.
Structured negotiation planning: objectives, leverage analysis, concession strategy, and scripted communication. The focus is disciplined decision-making and controlled messaging—so you don’t negotiate blind.
Mediation-ready case organization: chronologies, exhibits, key documents, and a settlement plan that anticipates counter-moves. Prepared parties consistently secure better outcomes.
Claim support when the process drifts: documentation packages, policy-language analysis, and dispute communications designed to preserve leverage and move matters toward resolution.
Practical risk control through contracts: limitation of liability, waivers, indemnity, insurance requirements, and dispute provisions—built to reduce exposure and improve predictability.
Evidence-forward evaluation and organization: fact development, documentation planning, record organization, and a clear theory of responsibility—so decisions are informed, strategic, and well-supported.
Structured evaluation of exposure narratives: timeline construction, source identification, medical record organization, and responsible-party mapping—designed for technical fact patterns.

Practice Areas

Service agreements, retainers, contractor terms, and commercial paperwork drafted with clarity and enforceability— focused on scope control, payment protection, and dispute provisions that work.
